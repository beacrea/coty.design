# coty.design — Content Strategy Refinement
**Direction:** Product Architecture & Principal Roles
**Constraint:** Single page, no portfolio, no case studies
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-16

---

## Core positioning problem

The current page works as a professional dossier and reads
authentically. The refinement needed is one of emphasis, not
structure. "Design and Product Leader" is accurate but positions
you as a generalist leader. The market roles that maximize
compensation — VP Product, Director of Product Design
(architecture-framed), Principal Product Architect,
Head of Product Architecture — reward signals of system
ownership, AI strategy, and operating model definition more
than craft or design org management.

The site should answer one question before anything else:
"What does Coty own and define?"
Currently it answers: "Who is Coty and what has he done?"
Both are valid. The first one pays more.

---

## Headline / Title

**Current:**
Coty Beasley — Design and Product Leader

**Directional:**
Coty Beasley — Product Architect & Co-Founder
or
Coty Beasley — Head of Product Architecture

**Reasoning:**
"Product Architect" is the highest-signal, shortest-path
label for the roles you're targeting. It front-loads
systems thinking and ownership rather than design
management. Co-Founder following it adds market
credibility that IC titles alone don't carry.

---

## Opening sentence / subhead

**Current framing (paraphrased):**
"Good design brings clarity to complexity and makes
technology genuinely useful."

**Directional:**
Keep the clarity-and-complexity idea but broaden it
beyond design:

> "I define how complex products fit together —
> aligning strategy, AI behavior, workflows, and
> interface rules so platforms scale without
> losing coherence."

**Reasoning:**
This is a product architecture statement, not a design
statement. It immediately signals principal-level
thinking and matches the language hiring managers
and recruiters use when filling Head of Product
Architecture and VP Product roles.

## "What I do" / Capabilities section

**Current approach:**
A list of professional modes and expertise domains,
which is comprehensive but reads as a long inventory.

**Directional:**
Trim to 5 operating capabilities, framed as things
you *define and own*, not skills you possess:

- Product architecture across multi-party, multi-system
  platforms
- AI-enabled workflow design and context engineering
- Semantic governance — canonical schemas, contracts,
  and interface specifications
- Design infrastructure (tokens, systems, component
  architecture, design-code alignment)
- Operating model definition for cross-functional
  product teams

**Reasoning:**
Five focused capabilities read as principal-level.
Ten broad domain labels read as generalist. The
current list is accurate and useful for AI/LLM
retrieval (keep it in llms.txt and llms-full.txt),
but on the visible page it diffuses the primary
signal you want human readers and recruiters to
take away.

## Biography / narrative block

**Current approach:**
Founder at Underline, two decades of product and
design leadership, democratic access theme,
computer science background.

**Directional:**
Keep the democratic access narrative — it is
genuinely distinctive and emotionally resonant —
but compress it to 2–3 sentences. Lead with the
present-tense role and scope before the history.

Example shape:
> "Co-founder at Underline, where I lead product
> strategy, AI innovation, and design engineering
> for the first privately funded open-access fiber
> network in the United States. My work across two
> decades has been shaped by one consistent idea:
> critical systems — infrastructure, finance, data —
> should be accessible to the people who depend on
> them."

**Reasoning:**
Current version leads with history. Directional
version leads with present scope and then earns
the backstory. That inversion is how principal
and executive candidates position themselves.

## Proof / signals section

**Current approach:**
Full press list, affiliations, published work topics,
and career timeline are all visible or near-visible
on the page.

**Directional:**
Collapse external validation into one short strip:

> Previously: VP Product, Neighborly (civic fintech,
> $25M Series A) · Co-Founder & Head of Product,
> Edge Up Sports × IBM Watson · Co-Founder, Underline
> ($100M Colorado Springs open-access network, Ares
> Management investment)
>
> Press: Wired · Fast Company · Bloomberg · NBC News ·
> Forbes · NPR

Keep the full press list and timeline in the
machine-readable layer (llms.txt / llms-full.txt)
where it has maximum value for AI retrieval without
cluttering the human reading path.

**Reasoning:**
For principal and VP-level roles, proof via brand-name
companies, dollar amounts, and press outlets works
faster than detailed narrative. One well-formatted
strip with Wired, Bloomberg, and $100M communicates
credibility in 3 seconds.

## Domain experience

**Current approach:**
Listed by vertical: fintech, civic, consumer
robotics, medical, networks, GIS, multimedia.

**Directional:**
Compress to a single sentence:
> "Sector experience across infrastructure, fintech,
> civic tech, enterprise, and AI systems."

Or keep as a short comma list with no sub-bullets.

**Reasoning:**
The long vertical list is strong for LLM/search
indexing but creates visual weight on the human
page. It also emphasizes breadth when you want
to signal depth in product architecture specifically.
Short reference is enough; the full detail lives
in the machine-readable layer.

---

## Contact / CTA

**Current approach:**
LinkedIn, GitHub, email available on request.

**Directional:**
Add one sentence that signals what kinds of
conversations you want:
> "Open to product architecture, principal product,
> and VP Product opportunities — remote."

**Reasoning:**
Stating the kind of conversation you want helps
both human readers (recruiters, hiring managers)
and AI systems matching your profile to roles.
It is a soft call-to-action that requires no
visual complexity.

---

## What stays the same

- Single page. No portfolio. No case studies.
- Plain, readable text-first layout.
- Tone: direct, not self-promotional.
- The opening philosophical sentence (refined but
  kept — it differentiates you from typical résumé-
  style pages).
- Machine-readable layer (llms.txt, llms-full.txt)
  continues to carry the full dossier detail. The
  visible page is the distilled signal; the data
  layer is the evidence.

---

## What moves to the data layer only

- Full career timeline
- Full press list with individual article citations
- Full professional affiliations list
- Full domain taxonomy (10+ verticals)
- Hackathon wins and early career entries

These remain canonically present on coty.design
via the machine-readable files — they are not
deleted, just moved off the primary human reading
path.
